The current problem is boring — and constant.

Kitchen trash bags are one of those products people buy forever without expecting them to improve. But the same complaints repeat every week in ordinary kitchens.

Too shortThey drop low in the can and stop reaching the rim.

You end up wrestling the bag over the top or buying a larger size than you really need.

Too tightStretching them into place can rip seams or corners.

The bag feels like it barely fits even though the box says it is the right size for your kitchen can.

Too slipperyAs soon as weight goes in, the liner can slide into the can.

That creates the annoying “trash under the bag” mess almost everyone recognizes instantly.

What GripFit would do differently

This is not a reinvention of waste management. It is a simple improvement to an old household product people already understand and already buy.

Questions people will ask

This page is meant to test whether the idea is compelling before manufacturing, so the most important thing is clarity.

Is this just a drawstring bag?

No — the core idea is a bag designed to fit and hold on to a regular kitchen can better, especially at the rim where the slipping problem starts.

Why would someone switch from a big brand?

Because the promise is more specific and more practical: not just “strong,” but “actually fits and stays put on my kitchen can.”

Why start with regular kitchen cans?

Because the category is common, easy to explain, and used constantly. That makes the problem broad and the buying behavior already familiar.
